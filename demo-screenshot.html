MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT
Between Acme Corporation ("Client") and TechVentures LLC ("Vendor") · Effective Date: March 1, 2026
1. Services

Vendor shall provide Client with software development and consulting services as described in one or more Statements of Work executed by the parties. Each Statement of Work shall be incorporated herein by reference and shall set forth the specific deliverables, timelines, and fees applicable to the engagement.

2. Term & Termination

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of one (1) year unless earlier terminated. Either party may terminate this agreement upon 3 days written notice for any reason. Upon expiration or termination, all outstanding invoices shall become immediately due and payable.

3. Fees & Payment

Client shall pay Vendor the fees set forth in each Statement of Work within thirty (30) days of receipt of Vendor's invoice. Late payments shall accrue interest at the rate of 3% per month, compounded monthly. Vendor reserves the right to suspend Services upon ten (10) days written notice for any overdue amounts exceeding sixty (60) days.

4. Intellectual Property

All work product, software, and deliverables created by Vendor under this Agreement shall remain the exclusive property of Vendor. Client receives only a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use such deliverables for its internal business purposes. Client acknowledges that Vendor may use similar deliverables for other clients.

5. Limitation of Liability

NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT. Vendor's total liability to Client under this Agreement shall not exceed One Hundred Dollars ($100), regardless of the nature of the claim.

6. Confidentiality

Each party agrees to keep confidential all non-public information of the other party for a period of one (1) year following disclosure. This obligation shall not apply to information that becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party, or that the receiving party independently develops without reference to the disclosing party's confidential information.

7. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. Any disputes arising hereunder shall be resolved by binding arbitration in accordance with the rules of the American Arbitration Association, with proceedings conducted in Wilmington, Delaware.
